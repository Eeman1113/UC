What's UC & Why Diet Matters? 🤔

Ulcerative Colitis (UC) is when your colon gets inflamed. While food doesn't cause UC, what you eat can really help manage symptoms and make you feel better! It's like giving your tummy a gentle hug. 🤗

SOS! Eating During a Flare-Up 🩹

When UC is active, your tummy needs extra TLC. The goal is to be gentle, reduce symptoms, and help your colon rest and heal. These tips are usually temporary!

Key Principles ✨

  • 🍚 Go for Low Fiber/Low Residue.
  • 🥣 Choose Soft Textures.
  • 💧 Stay Hydrated (lots of water!).
  • 💪 Get Enough Protein & Calories.
  • 🕰️ Eat Small, Frequent Meals.

Flare Diet Focus

Prioritize easily digestible foods during flares.

🥰 Foods to Enjoy

  • 🍌 Ripe Bananas, Melon, Applesauce
  • 🍑 Peeled/Cooked Fruits (Peaches, Pears)
  • 🥕 Cooked Carrots, Green Beans, Peeled Potatoes
  • 🍚 White Rice, White Pasta, White Bread
  • 🥣 Oatmeal, Cream of Wheat
  • 🐟 Lean Fish (Baked/Steamed)
  • 🐔 Skinless Poultry (Baked/Steamed)
  • 🥚 Eggs (Boiled, Poached, Scrambled)
  • 🥑 Smooth Avocado (in moderation)
  • 💧 Water, Clear Broths, Diluted Juices (no pulp)

😟 Foods to Limit/Avoid

  • 🍓 Raw Fruits with Skin/Seeds (Berries)
  • 🥦 Raw Veggies, Cruciferous (Broccoli, Cauliflower)
  • 🌽 Corn, Legumes (Beans, Lentils)
  • 🍞 Whole Grains, Breads with Seeds
  • 🍿 Popcorn, Nuts, Seeds
  • 🌶️ Spicy or Fried Foods
  • 🥓 Processed Meats
  • 🥛 Regular Dairy (if lactose intolerant)
  • ☕ Alcohol, Caffeine, Fizzy Drinks
  • 🍬 Sugary Sweets & Drinks

Yay! Eating During Remission 🎉

When symptoms are calm, it's time to nourish your body, maintain health, and keep those flares away! Gradually reintroduce foods.

Key Principles for Happy Remission 🥳

🌱Gradual Fiber Reintro
🥗Whole Foods Focus
✨Anti-Inflammatory
🌈Variety & Nutrients
💧Stay Hydrated
📝Mind Personal Triggers

🤩 Foods to Explore

  • 🍓 Wide Variety of Fruits (Berries, Apples)
  • 🥦 Colorful Veggies (Spinach, Sweet Potatoes)
  • 🌾 Whole Grains (Oats, Quinoa - if tolerated)
  • 🐟 Fatty Fish (Salmon, Mackerel - Omega-3s!)
  • 🐔 Lean Meats, Poultry, Eggs
  • 🫘 Legumes (Beans, Lentils - introduce slowly)
  • 🥛 Yogurt with Probiotics, Kefir
  • 🥑 Healthy Fats (Avocado, Olive Oil)
  • 🥜 Nuts & Seeds (in moderation, if tolerated)
  • 🍵 Herbal Teas, Water

🤨 Foods to Monitor

  • 🍟 Highly Processed Foods
  • 🥩 Red Meat (limit frequency)
  • 🥓 Processed Meats (sausages, bacon)
  • 🍩 Added Sugars & Sweets
  • 🍾 Excessive Alcohol
  • ❌ Known Personal Trigger Foods

Kitchen Magic: Prep & Texture Tips 🪄

How you cook can be as important as what you cook! These tricks can make foods easier on your tummy.

♨️

Cook Thoroughly

Softens food and makes it easier to digest.

🧑‍🍳

Peel & De-seed

Removes tough skins and seeds from fruits/veggies.

🥤

Blend & Puree

Great for smoothies and soups, making nutrients easy to absorb.

🍲

Gentle Cooking

Steam, boil, bake, or poach. Avoid frying.

Nutrient Power-Ups! 🔋⚡

UC can sometimes make it tricky to get all your nutrients. Focus on these superstars, especially if you've had a flare.

Iron 💪

For energy & to prevent anemia.

Find it in: Lean red meat, poultry, fish, eggs, cooked spinach, fortified cereals.

Calcium 🦴

For strong bones.

Find it in: Dairy/lactose-free options, fortified plant milks, leafy greens (cooked), tofu.

Vitamin D ☀️

Helps absorb calcium, supports immunity.

Find it in: Oily fish, egg yolks, fortified foods.

Protein 🍗

For healing and strength.

Find it in: Lean meats, fish, eggs, tofu, dairy/alternatives.

B Vitamins 💡

For energy and cell health.

Find it in: Meats, fish, eggs, dairy, fortified grains, leafy greens.

Hydration 💧

Super important, always!

Water, broths, oral rehydration solutions if needed.
